AMA Manual of Style (11th Edition) - Core Cheat Sheet

Target Agents: Agent 1 (Copy Editor)
Purpose: Enforce strict AMA style guidelines for medical formatting, abbreviations, numbers, and nomenclature.

1. Numbers and Percentages

Use numerals for expressing quantities, even those < 10 (e.g., 3 patients, 5 mL).

Exceptions: Spell out numbers that begin a sentence, title, subtitle, or heading (e.g., "Fifteen patients were admitted...").

Do not use a leading zero before a decimal point if the number cannot exceed 1 (e.g., p = .04, R2 = .85). Use a leading zero if the number can exceed 1 (e.g., 0.5 mg).

Express percentages with the % symbol (e.g., 5%). Do not include a space between the number and the symbol.

2. Units of Measure

Use the International System of Units (SI).

Always use a space between the number and the unit (e.g., 10 mg, 5 mm Hg).

Exception: No space for percentages, degrees, or mathematical operators (e.g., 5%, 37°C, p<.001).

3. Abbreviations and Acronyms

Do not use abbreviations in the title or abstract unless they are universally understood (e.g., DNA, HIV) or the term appears many times.

Expand all abbreviations at the first mention in the text, followed by the abbreviation in parentheses. E.g., "emergency department (ED)".

Do not define standard units of measure (e.g., kg, mL, mm Hg) or common statistical terms (e.g., SD, CI).

4. Eponyms vs. Non-eponymous Terms

Remove the possessive form for eponymous diseases, syndromes, and signs.

Correct: Down syndrome, Alzheimer disease, Babinski sign.

Incorrect: Down's syndrome, Alzheimer's disease, Babinski's sign.

5. Inclusive and Patient-First Language

Avoid labeling people by their diseases.

Use "patients with diabetes" instead of "diabetics".

Use "individuals with alcohol use disorder" instead of "alcoholics".

Avoid terms that suggest helplessness (e.g., "afflicted with", "suffering from", "victim of"). Use neutral phrasing ("diagnosed with", "affected by").

6. References Format (In-Text)

Use superscript Arabic numerals outside periods and commas, inside colons and semicolons.

E.g., "As reported previously,^1,3-5 this treatment is effective."
